TYPES OF CEMENT




  • Type 1- Ordinary Portland Cement [OPC]

    • Ordinary portland cement used for general purposes and most types of construction, most used type of cement and doesnt have special charectaristics (hence the name)
  • Type 2- Modified

    • low C3A [tricalcium aluminate] for moderate sulfate resistance
  • Type 3- Rapid hardening / High early strength

    • fine grinding
  • Type 4- Low heat

    • low C3S [tricalcium silicate] and low C3A [tricalcium aluminate] for low heat of hydration (minimises thermal cracking), useful in cold weather and or rapid construction
  • Type 5- Sulfate resistant

    • very low C3A [tricalcium aluminate] for high sulfate resistance

    sulfate attack- sulfate salts (from seawater or soil etc) breaks down the concrete decreases strength and causes cracks.

    thermal cracking- cracks in concrete caused due to temperature differences inside the concrete in large thick slabs moreso than smaller thinner ones.
